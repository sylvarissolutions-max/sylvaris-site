Data Processing Addendum

Effective April 11, 2026 Version 1.0

This Data Processing Addendum ("DPA") forms part of the services agreement between Sylvaris Solutions LLC ("Sylvaris", "Processor") and the Customer ("Controller") for the provision of Sylvaris products and services. It describes how Sylvaris processes personal data on behalf of the Controller and sets out the rights and obligations of both parties under applicable data protection laws.

This summary page is provided for reference. A fully executable DPA is issued as part of every commercial engagement and signed by both parties before any personal data is processed.

1. Definitions

Capitalized terms used in this DPA have the meanings given in applicable data protection laws, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the United Kingdom GDPR, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) where applicable. The terms "Personal Data", "Controller", "Processor", "Subprocessor", "Processing", and "Data Subject" carry their standard meanings under those laws.

2. Scope and role

Sylvaris acts as a Processor on behalf of the Controller for Personal Data processed in the course of delivering Sylvaris products and services. The Controller determines the purposes and means of processing. Sylvaris processes Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Controller, unless required to do otherwise by applicable law.

3. Processing activities

Subject matter

The provision of Sylvaris products and services as described in the applicable services agreement, including build, integration, operation, and support of AI systems for back-office, clinical, or other enterprise workflows.

Duration

For the term of the services agreement and any post-termination period required for data return or deletion.

Nature and purpose

Processing is limited to what is necessary to build, integrate, run, support, and maintain the Sylvaris product the Controller has engaged Sylvaris to deliver.

Categories of Data Subjects

  • Controller employees, contractors, and users of the Sylvaris product
  • Controller customers, patients, or end users whose data the Controller chooses to process through the Sylvaris product

Categories of Personal Data

Depending on the specific product and engagement, categories may include contact information, transactional records, support ticket content, clinical intake data, scheduling information, authentication credentials, and operational logs. The specific categories are documented in the applicable services agreement.

4. Processor obligations

  • Lawful processing. Sylvaris processes Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Controller and in accordance with applicable law.
  • Confidentiality. Personnel authorized to process Personal Data are bound by confidentiality obligations.
  • Security. Sylvaris implements appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect Personal Data against unauthorized or unlawful processing, loss, destruction, damage, or disclosure (see Section 6).
  • Assistance. Sylvaris assists the Controller in responding to Data Subject requests and in complying with the Controller's own obligations under applicable data protection laws.
  • No use of customer data for training. Sylvaris does not use Controller Personal Data to train, fine-tune, or improve AI models, and requires its subprocessors to operate under equivalent restrictions.
  • Return or deletion. On termination of the services agreement, Sylvaris returns or deletes all Personal Data at the Controller's choice, subject to legal retention requirements.

5. Subprocessors

Sylvaris engages subprocessors to provide hosting, model inference, communication, and other supporting services. All subprocessors are bound by contractual obligations materially equivalent to this DPA, including restrictions on using Controller data for model training.

A current list of subprocessors, including the services they provide and their locations, is made available to the Controller upon request under non-disclosure as part of the engagement. Sylvaris provides prior written notice of any intended addition or replacement of subprocessors. The Controller may object to a new subprocessor on reasonable grounds, in which case the parties will work in good faith to resolve the objection.

6. Security measures

Sylvaris maintains administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect Personal Data, including:

  • Encryption of Personal Data in transit and at rest using industry-standard protocols
  • Role-based access controls and the principle of least privilege
  • Authentication controls, including support for single sign-on and multi-factor authentication
  • Comprehensive audit logging of access to systems containing Personal Data
  • Network segmentation and firewall controls
  • Secure software development practices, including code review and dependency management
  • Regular review of security controls and incident response procedures
  • Personnel training on data protection and security

Specific technical and organizational measures for a given engagement are documented in the applicable services agreement.

7. International transfers

Sylvaris is based in the United States. Where Personal Data of individuals located in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or other jurisdictions with data export restrictions is transferred to the United States or other third countries, Sylvaris relies on appropriate legal mechanisms, including the Standard Contractual Clauses adopted by the European Commission, the UK International Data Transfer Addendum, and equivalent mechanisms where applicable.

8. Data incident notification

Sylvaris notifies the Controller without undue delay, and in any event within seventy-two (72) hours, after becoming aware of a Personal Data breach affecting Controller data. The notification includes, to the extent known at the time, the nature of the incident, categories and approximate number of Data Subjects affected, likely consequences, and measures taken or proposed.

9. Audit rights

Sylvaris makes available to the Controller information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA. The Controller may request an audit, which may be satisfied by providing written responses to a reasonable security questionnaire or, for Enterprise engagements, by a remote or on-site audit conducted at mutually agreed times and at the Controller's expense.

10. Return and deletion

On termination or expiry of the services agreement, and at the Controller's choice, Sylvaris will either return Personal Data to the Controller or delete Personal Data from Sylvaris systems, subject to any legal retention requirements. A confirmation of deletion is provided on request.

11. Liability

The liability of each party under this DPA is governed by the limitation of liability provisions in the underlying services agreement.

12. Order of precedence

In the event of a conflict between this DPA and the underlying services agreement, this DPA prevails with respect to the processing of Personal Data.

13. Governing law

This DPA is governed by the same law as the underlying services agreement, except where mandatory data protection law requires otherwise.
